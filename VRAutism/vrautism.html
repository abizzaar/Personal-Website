Abizar

What if the lights felt so bright that they hurt your eyes and made your head ache?

We designed a virtual reality experience that stimulates the different senses of our user. It is an exploration of using technology in therapy.

Project Description

The task was to design an immersive virtual reality experience to replace traditional autism therapy. We had 3 months to design the experience with help from professors at Northwestern University.

Client

TherapyOS, a VR healthcare startup

What I did

The task was to design an immersive virtual reality experience to replace traditional autism therapy. We had 3 months to design the experience with help from professors at Northwestern University.

Our User (Ethan Ducayet)

Because autism has such a large spectrum, we wanted to focus on one specific user. Ethan Ducayet turned out to be an ideal candidate for many reasons. We were really grateful for his cooperation.

Ethan loves technology and was willing to try on the VR headset.

Ethan is able to navigate new experiences.

Ethan has great attention to detail and an excellent memory.

Ethan was able to give us tangible feedback through surveys.
